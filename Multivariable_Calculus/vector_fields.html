Vector Fields

A vector field is something of the form:

\[ \vec{F} = M\hat{i} + N\hat{j} \]

where \(M\) and \(N\) are functions of \(x\) and \(y\). Concretely, this means that for each point in the plane \((x,y)\), you have a vector \(\vec{F}\) that depends on \((x,y)\): hence, a vector field or a field of vectors.
